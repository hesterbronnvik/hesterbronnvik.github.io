Research Insight

I am interested in how, given internal state, external environment, and current goals, animals use and move through their multi-layered environments to balance their individual costs and benefits. I am particularly focused on fitness effects through mortality and reproductive success.

Profile

If there is one optimal state for a living organism—one best form or best behavior—why do we observe great variation among individuals?

Traditionally, behavioral ecology has answered this question by invoking environmental variation. For example, we can expect parents holding different territories to raise different numbers of offspring based on food availability.

In my doctoral research, I expand on this by examining variation within individuals—the change in optimal behavior with age. I have focused on long-distance migration as a model behavior.
